01 — Building Code (Planning & Building Code)

1.1 Decree No. (45) of 2021 — Dubai Building Code

WhatDecree by the Ruler of Dubai establishing the unified Dubai Building Code (DBC) as a mandatory standard
Why neededLegal foundation: gives DBC the force of law. Without this decree, DBC would be merely advisory
Key pointsApplies to ALL projects in Dubai, including free zones and DIFC. Repeals all conflicting standards (Art. 8)
Size4 pages, 9 articles
StatusActive since 18 December 2021
DownloadDownload PDF (414 KB)
Official sourceDubai Legal Portal (dlp.dubai.gov.ae)

1.2 Dubai Building Code 2021 (full technical code)

WhatThe complete Dubai Building Code: architecture, structural, fire safety, energy efficiency, accessibility, MEP (Parts A–K)
Why neededTechnical requirements for design — no building permit without DBC compliance
Key pointsUnified code replacing fragmented regulations. Enacted per Decree No. 45/2021. Parts: General, Architecture, Accessibility, Vertical Transport, Envelope, Structure, Utilities, Indoor, Security, Villas
Size500+ pages, ~31 MB
StatusActive

02 — Energy Efficiency

2.1 Al Sa’fat — Dubai Green Building System (v2.0)

WhatDubai green building evaluation system: mandatory requirements for energy efficiency, water conservation, resources and indoor environment
Why neededMandatory for all buildings >500 m² (since 2017). Defines rating (1–5 Sa’fat Stars) and minimum requirements for Building Permit
Key points33 mandatory requirements (envelope, equipment, renewables); reference building model; integrated into Dubai Building Code
Size~78 pages
StatusActive (updated 2020+)
DownloadDownload PDF (2.4 MB)
Official sourceMinistry of Energy & Infrastructure (moei.gov.ae)

03 — Building Permits

3.1 Administrative Resolution No. (37) of 2021 — Building Requirements & Specifications

WhatAmendments to the main Bylaw on building requirements and specifications (Administrative Resolution 125/2001). Detailed technical standards
Why neededDefines specific requirements for Building Permit: geotechnical, reinforcement, structural calculations
Key pointsBorehole requirements (1 per 750 m²), minimum reinforcement (1% columns, 0.4% walls), updated specifications
Size38 pages
StatusActive
DownloadDownload PDF (1.2 MB)
Official sourceDubai Legal Portal (dlp.dubai.gov.ae)

04 — Zoning & Land Use

4.1 Local Order No. (2) of 1999 — Classification and Regulation of Land Use

WhatFundamental zoning law for Dubai: land classification, permitted uses, Planning Permit system
Why neededDefines what can be built on a specific plot. No permit without zoning compliance
Key pointsPermitted Uses / Conditional Uses / Non-compliant Uses. Zone maps: R1–R9 (residential), C1–C2 (commercial), etc.
Size95 pages
StatusActive (base act, amended)
DownloadDownload PDF (753 KB)
Official sourceDubai Legal Portal (dlp.dubai.gov.ae)

05 — Engineering Practice

5.1 Administrative Resolution No. (253) of 2021 — Technical Qualification for Engineers

WhatTechnical qualification requirements and criteria for engineers working in construction in Dubai
Why neededMandatory for DEQS registration (Dubai Engineers Register). Cannot submit permit applications without it
Key pointsQualification requirements (UAE-licensed engineers), categorization by specialty and grade, NOC for branches
Size8 pages
StatusActive
DownloadDownload PDF (531 KB)
Official sourceDubai Legal Portal (dlp.dubai.gov.ae)

06 — Fire & Life Safety

6.1 UAE Fire and Life Safety Code of Practice (2017 Edition)

WhatComplete UAE Fire Code by Civil Defence: fire prevention, materials/equipment, occupancies, high-rise requirements, structural fire resistance, emergency plans
Why neededMandatory at all stages (design, construction, occupancy). Civil Defence NOC required for all projects
Key pointsBuilding classification including «super high-rise» (>90m), fire risk assessments, fire ratings for structural elements, evacuation requirements
Size200+ pages, ~311 MB (scanned document)
StatusActive (2017 edition, 2020 updates)

07 — Contractor Licensing

7.1 Law No. (7) of 2025 — Regulating Contracting Activities in Dubai

WhatNew law on contracting activities: unified Contractor Register via Dubai Municipality, contractor classification (grades), registration requirements
Why neededMandatory for all contractors in Dubai, including free zones and DIFC. Replaces the old licensing system
Key pointsUnified contractor register, classification by grades and financial/technical capabilities, transition period until 8 January 2027
StatusNew published 08.07.2025, effective 08.01.2026
PDF not available for direct download. Full text available in HTML format:
1. Go to: Dubai Legal Portal → search «Law No. 7 of 2025»
2. Detailed legal review: Al Tamimi & Company
